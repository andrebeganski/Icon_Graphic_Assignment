Football may be a game of inches, but touchdowns reign supreme. They represent the perfect outcome to an offensive drive and are the building blocks by which teams construct a win. By looking at the leading players who have scored touchdowns in different ways we can better understand who is performing at the top of the league. Fantasy football fans will understand the value of these rankings, but they’re sure to change as the season progresses.

In this article we’ll divide offensive touchdowns into three categories and look at the trends that emerge on an individual player level and across teams.

 
Passing

It’s no surprise that star-player Patrick Mahomes leads the league in passing touchdowns at this point in the season. The Kansas City Chiefs have been red-hot on offense and posted some of the best numbers in terms of stats that football fans have seen in a while. It’s worth noting that both Tom Brady and Patrick Mahomes played each other in the Super Bowl last year. They are both two quarterbacks who have been consistently great throughout their careers and don’t show any signs of regressing this year.

Justin Herbert was a rookie last year and it’s surprising to see him ranked among the best quarterbacks in the league with 13 total touchdowns for the season. Brandon Stanley, who serves as Herbert’s coach and was hired this past offseason, has had no problems adjusting to the NFL in his first year as a coach in the league. His offense has been firing on all cylinders.

Rushing

Derick Henry has been virtually unstoppable this year, averaging over 120 rushing yards in each game so far this season. Henry held the record last year for the most yards rushed during the regular season at 2,027 yards while scoring 17 touchdowns. If he continues at his current break-neck pace he will shatter his previous records. As fantasy managers say, “you never fade the Big Dog.”

It is shocking for everyone to see Sam Darnold tied for the second most rushing touchdowns at this point in the season, or any point, for that matter. In his first year with the Carolina Panthers after struggling on the New York Jets, his performance so far this year is a clear testament to just how bad his former team was. He may be a rushing weapon in the red zone right now, but as teams figure out how to play against Darnold, he will start to slide towards the back of the pack.

It’s worth noting Austin Ekeler’s appearance on this list. Playing on the same team as Just Herbert, he is the second Charger to appear in these rankings.

Receiving

When it comes to ranking wide receivers, there is less distinction when looking at touchdowns caught during the season, overall. Mike Williams, the third Charger in this article, is the clear leader, scoring 6 touchdowns so far this season. His positioning within the list shows how his team has outperformed in all areas of offense.

Tight end Rob Gronkowski and wide receiver Mike Evans both make an appearance in this category. These players have benefitted from Tom Brady’s performance thus far, and will likely stay on this list, if not, move up it further.

You see a similar trend in the rankings of Travis Kelce and Tyreek Hill. Both players have elevated stats because of how good Patrick Mahomes has been playing this season. Yet, despite their monster performances, the two players account for less than half of the touchdowns Patrick Mahomes has scored so far this year.

Ja’Marr Chase is the only rookie on this entire list and it’s shocking to see him outpacing other established receivers. He has played only five games so far this season and has averaged a touchdown in each appearance. For fantasy football fans, if Chase is still available in your league you NEED to pick him up. My eyes are on him for offensive rookie of the year given how he’s played so far.
